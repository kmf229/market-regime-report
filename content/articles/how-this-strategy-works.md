This rules-based trading system measures whether risk-on assets (tech, small caps) or risk-off assets (utilities, gold, treasuries) demonstrate market leadership, then aligns positions accordingly. Everything is rules-based, systematic, and objective—no predictions or discretionary overrides.

## Key Characteristics

### Frequency & Duration

- Regime changes occur approximately 5-6 times annually
- Average position duration spans 2-3 months
- Infrequent, stable signals designed for swing trading rather than frequent trading

### Two Position States

**Bullish Regime:** Traders may select MNQ futures, TQQQ, or QQQ depending on risk tolerance

**Bearish Regime:** Options include MGC futures, GLD, or cash holdings for protection

## Target Audience

The strategy suits professionals with limited availability, as daily monitoring suffices given infrequent signal changes. This contrasts sharply with attention-demanding trading systems requiring constant screen time.

## Strengths & Limitations

### Advantages

- Clear rules with no ambiguity
- No entertainment value distraction
- Efficient time requirements
- Natural alignment with bull trends
- Defensive positioning during rising risk

### Disadvantages

- Lacks individual stock selection
- Potentially feels slow during low-volatility periods
- Demands discipline during drawdowns
