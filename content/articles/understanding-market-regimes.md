Markets don't behave the same way all the time. Sometimes they reward risk-taking. Other times they punish it severely. Understanding which regime you're in is crucial for portfolio positioning.

## What Is a Market Regime?

A market regime is a sustained period where certain asset classes, factors, or strategies tend to outperform. Think of it as the market's "mood"—except this mood can last for months or years.

The two primary regimes:

**Risk-On**: Markets favor growth, momentum, and higher-beta assets. Investors are rewarded for taking risk. Correlations between risk assets stay low. Volatility is contained.

**Risk-Off**: Markets favor safety, quality, and defensive assets. Risk-taking is punished. Correlations spike. Volatility expands.

## Why Regimes Matter

Most portfolios are built for one regime: the bull market. They hold stocks, maybe some bonds, and hope for the best. This works great during risk-on periods but can be devastating during regime shifts.

Consider 2022:
- Stocks fell 20%+
- Bonds fell 13%
- The traditional 60/40 portfolio had its worst year in decades

Investors who recognized the regime shift early and rotated to defensive positioning avoided much of this drawdown.

## Identifying Regimes

You don't need complex models to identify regimes. Some simple indicators:

### Market Leadership
- Are growth stocks leading or lagging?
- Is tech outperforming utilities?
- Are small caps beating large caps?

### Trend
- Are major indices above or below their moving averages?
- Is the trend up, down, or sideways?

### Volatility
- Is the VIX elevated or contained?
- Are daily swings getting larger?

### Credit Spreads
- Are spreads widening or tightening?
- Is the bond market signaling stress?

## The Regime Model Approach

Our regime model synthesizes multiple indicators into a single signal: Risk-On or Risk-Off.

When Risk-On: Position in leveraged growth exposure (TQQQ/MNQ)

When Risk-Off: Rotate to gold (GLD/MGC)

This binary approach keeps things simple. No partial positions, no hedge overlays, no complexity. You're either positioned for risk or positioned for safety.

## Regime Changes Are Infrequent

One key insight: regimes don't change often. We typically see just a handful of regime shifts per year. This means:

- You don't need to check your portfolio daily
- Transaction costs stay low
- The strategy works for busy professionals
- Emotional decision-making is minimized

## Conclusion

Understanding market regimes won't help you predict the future. But it will help you identify the present environment and position appropriately.

The goal isn't to time the market perfectly. It's to be on the right side of major trends and avoid the catastrophic drawdowns that occur when you're positioned wrong for the regime.
